What are Cookies?
Cookies are small text files which save internet settings. Almost all websites make use of Cookies. They are downloaded by your web browser during your first visit to a website. The next time that you visit the website with the same device, the cookie (and the information that it holds) will either be transmitted back to the website that created it (so-called first party Cookies) or to a third-party website if the cookie is from that website (so-called third party Cookies). This allows the website to know that it has already been accessed by the browser in question; in some circumstances, the website may vary the content that is displayed.
